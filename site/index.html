Nine thingsit does.

And one long list of things it doesn't — like sell you out.

I.

Scan in seconds

Point at any receipt. Apple's Vision framework extracts the store, date, and total on-device. No photos leave your phone.

II.

Email receipts, too

Share any digital receipt from Mail — Amazon, Apple, Best Buy, and anything else via Apple Intelligence — parsed on-device in one tap. No cloud round-trip.

III.

106 return policies

A built-in database of retailer return windows means the right deadline appears the moment you save. Offline, always.

IV.

Timed reminders

Nudges at 14 days, 7 days, 3 days, and the morning of. Quiet when safe. Loud when it matters. Live Activity on your lock screen when a return is three days out.

V.

Return Mode

Heading to the store? One screen shows the countdown, the map, the policy, and what to bring. Arm's length legible.

VI.

Proof-of-return capture

When you mark an item returned, snap a photo of the return-counter receipt and jot a note. Your paper trail if the retailer ever disputes it.

VII.

Tap-to-return widget

Lock-screen widgets are interactive: mark an item returned without opening the app. Siri and Spotlight search both work, too.

VIII.

Native on Mac

A true Mac app — not a Catalyst port. Sidebar navigation, menu-bar commands, Finder drag-and-drop. Syncs with your iPhone through your iCloud.

IX.

Household sharing

Opt-in, per-receipt. Shared items appear in a dedicated section of your partner's vault; changes mirror across devices in seconds via silent CloudKit push. Runs through your iCloud, not through us.
