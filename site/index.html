Return windows are small. Your memory is smaller. Receipt Folder closes the gap.

You bought the speakers. They're fine. But two months in, the left channel hisses. You go to return them. The clerk says "our policy is 30 days." You check your email. It's been forty-two.

Receipt Folder is a small, disciplined notebook for every one of those deadlines. It reads them off the receipt when you buy, reminds you before the window closes, and quietly forgets them when the warranty expires.

Six thingsit does.

And one long list of things it doesn't — like sell you out.

I.

Scan in seconds

Point at any receipt. Apple's Vision framework extracts the store, date, and total on-device. No photos leave your phone.

II.

Return windows tracked

A built-in database of 100+ retailer policies means the right deadline appears the moment you save.

III.

Timed reminders

Nudges at 14 days, 7 days, 3 days, and the morning of. Quiet when safe. Loud when it matters.

IV.

Warranty watch

Set once. We remind you 90, 30, and 7 days before expiry so you can inspect while you're still covered.

V.

Return Mode

Heading to the store? One screen shows the countdown, the map, the policy, and what to bring. Arm's length legible.

VI.

All on your phone

No servers. No accounts. No analytics. CloudKit syncs to your own iCloud if you want it. That's the only network we touch.
